Web Spectrogram

Used as a support for the vocal coach, this spectrogram aims to extract parameters from the spectrum of a live voice to analyze its fundamental characteristics. The vocal coach can thus study the volume, the fundamental frequency of the voice, the main formants and guide the singer in his training. To accomplish this mission, I developed a mesh linking several open source signal processing libraries that draw from each other to yield the characteristics requested by the client. The main tasks were to:

  • Allow voice recording
  • Extract the fundamental frequency, the first two formants of the signal for each record, and a tool for comparison of the latter with the generic formants of each vowel
  • Determine the type of voice used (chest or head)
  • Create a downloadable profile of the voice's range

  • Technologies: HMTL5, Polymer, Javascript
  • Client: DS Vocology - Los Angeles, Californie, USA
  • Date: December 2016
  • Service: Web Development, Spectral analysis

Spectrogramme Web

Soutien au coach vocal, ce spectrogramme vise à extraire des paramètres du spectre de la voix en direct pour en analyser des caractéristiques fondamentales. Le coach vocal peut ainsi étudier le volume, la fréquence fondamentale de la voix, les formantes principales et guider le chanteur dans son entrainement. Pour accomplir cette mission, j'ai élaboré un maillage entre plusieurs libraires javascript open source de traitement de signal qui tirent chacune l'une de l'autre pour donner les caractéristiques demandées par le client. Les principales tâches accomplies furent :

  • Permettre d'enregistrer la voix
  • Dégager la fréquence fondamentale, les deux premières formantes du signal pour chaque enregistrement et un outil de comparaison de ces dernières avec les formantes génériques de chaque voyelles
  • Déterminer le type de voix utilisé (de poitrine ou de tête)
  • Dresser un profil de l'étendu de la voix

  • Technologies: HMTL5, Polymer, Javascript
  • Client: DS Vocology - Los Angeles, Californie, USA
  • Date: Décembre 2016
  • Service: Développement Web, Analyse spectrale

Allegory of a choreographer and his interpreters

This artistic project was realized as part of an interpretation course at École de Danse Contemporaine de Montréal . I was led to perform a dance creation and create a piece of art inspired by it. The bubble embodies the dance studio where all the creative magic takes place. The human being is the choreographer, and all his shadows on the bubble are the dancers, who act as projections of his ideas. The lights represent dance proposals that are preserved (red), and those that come and go (in green) throughout the creative process. The buzzing sound recalls the calm tension I tried to emulate while dancing the studio.

  • Technologies: C++, OpenGL, OpenScenegraph
  • Date: December 2015

"Environnements susceptibles"

This work was done as part of my end-of-studies project at the École de Technologie Supérieure (MTI 835) . The project "Environnements susceptibles" is a research project created by Société des Arts Technologiques aiming at studying the notions of ephemeral and the presence of humans in the environment. It allows users to degrade the environment, leave traces and interact with each other. This collaboration with the Metallab of the Société des Arts Technologiques lead to the development of a prototype laying the ground work for "Environnements susceptibles".

I had to design an interactive and degradable environment based on OpenGL and written in C ++. I conducted research in the field of environmental degradation in the virtual world. I was able to model the notions of ephemeral and the sense of presence visually in the form of footprints. The interface offered several means of interaction with the user. Then I had to manage constraints of display, of information storage and of real time. The main special effect technique I used were the bump mapping and the performance tool I developped included the transferring of information to the graphics card through encoded textures.

  • Technologies: C++, OpenGL, OpenScenegraph, Shaders, QT
  • Client: Société des Arts Technologiques, Montréal, Québec, Canada
  • Date: July 2013
